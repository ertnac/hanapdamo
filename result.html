About

Oregano is a fragrant, bushy perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is widely recognized for its strong, earthy flavor and is a staple in many culinary traditions.

Beyond the kitchen, it has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its high concentration of antioxidants and potent essential oils.

Health Benefits

Oregano is highly valued for its versatile medicinal applications. Traditionally, its leaves are boiled into a warm tea or extract to provide respiratory relief, helping to soothe coughs, colds, sore throats, and asthma.

Furthermore, the plant contains active compounds like carvacrol and thymol that offer strong antimicrobial properties, assisting the body in fighting off bacterial and fungal infections.

Beyond respiratory and immune support, consuming oregano also acts as an effective digestive aid to help relieve common issues such as bloating, indigestion, and upset stomachs.

Growing Guide

Oregano thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is a highly resilient plant that is drought-tolerant once established, making it very easy to grow in tropical climates like the Philippines.

Watering: Only water when the top inch of soil is completely dry. Overwatering can cause root rot.

Harvesting: Regular trimming encourages bushier growth. For maximum medicinal potency, harvest leaves just before the plant blooms.
